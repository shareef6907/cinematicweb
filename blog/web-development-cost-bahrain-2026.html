What Affects Website Pricing

Understanding what drives costs helps you make smarter decisions. Here are the key factors:

1. DESIGN COMPLEXITY

A simple template adaptation costs far less than a completely custom design. Template-based sites use pre-made layouts with your branding applied. Custom designs are created from scratch specifically for your brand, with unique layouts, interactions, and visual elements that no one else has.

2. DEVELOPMENT APPROACH

There's a massive difference between a WordPress site and a custom-coded website. WordPress is faster and cheaper initially but comes with limitations, security concerns, and ongoing plugin costs. Custom code gives you complete control, better performance, and no dependencies on third-party plugins.

3. FUNCTIONALITY REQUIREMENTS

A brochure site with static content costs far less than a site with:

  • User registration and login systems
  • Booking or reservation systems
  • Payment processing
  • Real-time features
  • Custom calculators or tools
  • API integrations

4. CONTENT CREATION

Many quotes assume you'll provide all content (text, images, videos). If you need:

  • Professional copywriting: Add BD 200-500
  • Professional photography: Add BD 200-600
  • Video production: Add BD 500-3,000+

5. SEO AND MARKETING

Basic SEO (proper titles, meta descriptions, site structure) should be included in any professional website. Advanced SEO strategy, keyword research, and content optimization add to the cost but dramatically improve your return on investment.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Many businesses get caught off-guard by costs that weren't in the original quote. Here's what to ask about:

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Hidden Costs:

HOSTING

Some developers quote low prices but don't include hosting. Bahrain hosting costs typically range from BD 50-200 per year for standard sites, more for high-traffic or e-commerce.

SSL CERTIFICATES

Essential for security and SEO. Some are free (Let's Encrypt), premium ones cost BD 30-100/year. Ask if SSL is included.

DOMAIN REGISTRATION

Your .com, .bh, or other domain costs BD 10-50/year depending on the extension. Someone needs to manage this.

ONGOING MAINTENANCE

WordPress sites especially need regular updates for security. Budget BD 100-300/year minimum for maintenance, or you're gambling with your site's security.

CONTENT UPDATES

Will you update the site yourself? If not, how much does the agency charge for changes? Some charge hourly (BD 25-75/hour), some offer monthly retainers (BD 50-200/month).

REVISIONS DURING DEVELOPMENT

Most quotes include 2-3 rounds of revisions. Beyond that, expect additional charges. Clarify this before signing.

PLUGIN LICENSES

WordPress premium plugins often have annual fees. These add up: a forms plugin, SEO plugin, security plugin, backup plugin... suddenly you're paying BD 200+/year in plugin renewals.

Here's our honest advice on how to approach your website budget:

FOR STARTUPS AND SMALL BUSINESSES

If you're just starting out and need basic online presence, a BD 500-800 template-based site can work. Just understand its limitations and plan to upgrade within 2-3 years as you grow.

If your business is established and your website is a significant customer touchpoint, invest BD 1,500-3,000 minimum. A poor website actively loses you customers. The ROI on a good website is typically 200-400% within the first year.
