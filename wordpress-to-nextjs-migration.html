Why Migrate from WordPress to Next.js?

WordPress powers 40% of the web — but that doesn't mean it's the best choice for your business. If you're struggling with slow page loads, constant security updates, expensive managed hosting, or an outdated design, it might be time to upgrade.

Next.js is a modern React framework that generates lightning-fast static websites. Your pages are pre-built at deploy time, served from a global CDN, and load in milliseconds. No database queries, no PHP execution, no security patches — just pure speed.

Our Migration Process

We've migrated dozens of WordPress sites to Next.js. Here's how we ensure a smooth transition:

01

Site Audit & Planning

We analyze your current WordPress site: pages, posts, URLs, plugins, and functionality. We map out exactly what needs to be rebuilt and plan the migration timeline.

02

Content Export & Restructure

We export all your content and organize it for the new system. This might be Markdown files, a headless CMS, or even keeping WordPress as a headless backend.

03

Design & Development

We rebuild your site in Next.js — either matching your current design or upgrading to a new look. Every page, every component, optimized for speed.

04

SEO Preservation

We set up 301 redirects for all URLs, migrate meta tags and schema markup, and ensure Google sees the new site as a continuation of the old one.

05

Testing & Launch

Comprehensive testing across devices and browsers. We verify all redirects work, all content is in place, and performance meets our standards before going live.

What About Content Editing?

One common concern: "I like how easy WordPress is to edit." Good news — you don't have to give that up. We have multiple options:

  • Headless WordPress — Keep using the WordPress admin panel for editing. Next.js pulls content from WordPress via API. Best of both worlds.
  • Headless CMS (Sanity, Contentful) — Modern editing interfaces designed for structured content. Often easier than WordPress.
  • Notion as CMS — For simpler sites, we can use Notion as your content database. Edit in Notion, site updates automatically.
  • Markdown + Git — For tech-savvy teams, content lives in markdown files. Version controlled and developer-friendly.

What Happens to My Plugins?

Plugin functionality is rebuilt natively or replaced with better alternatives:

  • Yoast/RankMath → Built-in Next.js SEO components (better control, no bloat)
  • Contact Form 7 → Serverless form handlers (more reliable, no spam)
  • WooCommerce → Shopify or custom e-commerce (faster, more features)
  • Page Builders → React components (cleaner code, better performance)
  • Caching Plugins → Not needed (sites are pre-built and cached by default)

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I migrate from WordPress to Next.js?
Next.js offers significant advantages: 10x faster page loads (improving SEO and conversions), no database or PHP vulnerabilities to patch, much lower hosting costs ($0-20/month vs $50-200/month for managed WordPress), better Core Web Vitals scores, and a modern development experience that makes future updates easier.
How much does WordPress to Next.js migration cost?
Migration costs depend on site complexity. A simple brochure website (5-10 pages) costs BD 500-800. A medium business site with blog (20-50 pages) costs BD 800-1,500. Complex sites with e-commerce, membership, or custom functionality range BD 1,500-3,000+. We provide exact quotes after reviewing your current site.
Will I lose my SEO rankings after migration?
No — when done properly, migration actually improves SEO. We implement proper 301 redirects for all URLs, preserve all meta data and structured markup, and the performance improvements from Next.js typically boost rankings. We've seen clients gain rankings after migration due to improved Core Web Vitals.
How long does migration take?
A typical migration takes 2-4 weeks depending on site size. Week 1: audit and planning. Week 2-3: development and content migration. Week 4: testing, redirects, and launch. Complex sites may take 4-6 weeks. We never rush migrations — proper execution is critical for preserving SEO.
Can I still update content easily without WordPress?
Yes! We can set up a headless CMS (like Sanity, Contentful, or even headless WordPress) that gives you an easy editing interface while serving your site through Next.js. For simpler sites, we can use Markdown files or Notion as a content source. You won't lose the ability to edit your content.
What happens to my WordPress plugins?
Plugin functionality is rebuilt natively in Next.js or replaced with better alternatives. Contact forms become serverless functions. SEO plugins are replaced with Next.js SEO components. Analytics and tracking are implemented directly. Most plugin functionality can be replicated with better performance and no security risks.
